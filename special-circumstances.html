Can Tis the Season Do Something During the Corona Virus Pandemic?

Please read below and reach out to us.

Ryan to Stetson - 11am

"[A Tis the Season Customer] just sent me this."

Stetson to Ryan - 11:02am

"That's crazy man. If I'm a homeowner, the last thing I'm thinking about is putting my holiday lights up."

Ryan to Stetson - 11:38am

"The news [KMBC 9] is now sharing this."

These are actual text messages between Ryan and I this morning, March 18th. We, like I'm certain many of you, are mostly staying at home, waiting, and watching as the COVID-19 pandemic unfolds. In February and early March, Ryan and I's other company Great Plains Painting was ramping up and we were starting to get busy. In the last week, we've seen a sharp drop off in traffic to our website and incoming painting estimate requests, and understandably so. We've changed up the way we are operating (detailed here for those curious) so that we're keeping our team safe and also doing what we can to limit community spread. I'm sure many local small businesses are feeling the slow down we are.

After Ryan sent me the second message, I called him and we talked about the possibility of hanging Christmas lights in March. As we discussed we decided on an idea we are trying, outlined below

  • If you have lights from us cost to rehang is $1.25/linear feet.

    We have your linear foot or it is on your invoice from 2019. Example for a 100 foot house the price is $125. Includes install and takedown.

  • We're donating $0.25/linear feet of the cost to our local Harvesters

  • If you choose to work with us, we'll install your lights without any contact to you or your family, just like we do during the holiday season.

  • We're doing lights only, no wreaths.

  • We plan on leaving the lights up until April 15th, but are happy to work with you on schedule.

Now, we're running this in response to what we saw online and to help supplement our income as small business owners during this slow time for our painting company. We completely understand however if there is 0 demand for holiday lighting in March during a virus pandemic. If you are also unable to work at this time, and are conserving your finances to pay your bills and provide for yourself and your family, we get it!

We thank you for your support and hope all of you stay safe during this time. If you're interested the easiest way to reach us is via text message to 8559090717.

Tis the Season - Ryan, Stetson, and Angela
